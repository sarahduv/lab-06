FRYDAY, FISHTOBER 8, 2099
Salmon Cookie Monster Strikes Again

Thieves who tied up a family of four inside their Codd County home, all the while accomplices cleaned out the family's cookie jars may have made off with as much as $10 million in the prized salmon cookies, according to Channel 2 Action News.

Two men wearing lobster masks followed the father of the Guppy family to his home the night of Fishtober 6, forced him inside and cleaned out the cookie jars, police said. Investigators said the father was ambushed after returning from fishing at a nearby pond, the news station reported. The family was bound with duct tape and zip ties as the thieves robbed their cookie jars.

Two of the home's seven cabinets were cleared out and an undetermined amount of salmon cookies were stolen, according to an Seattle police report. Investigators said on Clamday the family has still not provided them with an itemized list of the stolen cookies, nor have they handed over the surveillance footage. The thieves had a full two hours to eat what they wanted, according to the report, which indicates the men didn’t leave until about 1:30 a.m.

Two men captured on the home's surveillance footage appear to be speaking on FaceTime as they used crow bars to open the cabinet doors which were holding the

salmon cookies, according to the report. The police did not learn of the cookie crime until an hour after they left.

“Other than the kitchen cabinets, there was no other damage to the home itself. So they obviously knew exactly what they wanted to do and

were able to execute it,” Seattle police spokesman Officer Haddock told the news station.

No arrests have been made in the case. “We will not rest until we solve this unspeakable cookie crime”, Seattle police Sgt. Pollock said.
